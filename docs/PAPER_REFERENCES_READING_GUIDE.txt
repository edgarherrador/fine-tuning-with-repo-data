#1: ExpRL — Exploratory RL for LLM Mid-Training
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ArXiv ID: 2606.17024
Title: "ExpRL: Exploratory RL for LLM Mid-Training"
Authors: Violet Xiang, Amrith Setlur, Chase Blagden, Nick Haber, Aviral Kumar
Published: June 15, 2026

Abstract: 
Sparse reward reinforcement learning has become a standard tool for improving 
LLM reasoning, but its success depends critically on the coverage present in 
the base model. In practice, models are often primed for RL through mid-training 
on curated reasoning traces that teach useful primitives. ExpRL proposes a 
curriculum learning approach using change-driven training.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR YOUR WORK:
├─ "Mid-training on curated traces" = your merged commits
├─ Curriculum ordering = commits by complexity (simple→complex)
├─ "Teach useful primitives" = repo coding conventions

#2: ContextRL — Context-Aware RL for Agentic and Multimodal LLMs

ArXiv ID: 2606.17053
Title: "Context-Aware RL for Agentic and Multimodal LLMs"
Authors: Peiyang Xu, Bangzheng Li, Sijia Liu, Karthik R. Narasimhan, 
         Pramod Viswanath, Prateek Mittal, Xingyu Fu
Published: June 15, 2026

Abstract:
Large language models often fail when answering requires identifying a small 
but decisive piece of evidence within a long or complex context, such as a 
single line in a tool trace or a subtle detail in an image. We propose 
ContextRL, a context-aware reinforcement learning method that improves the 
model's ability to attend to relevant context.

#3: HAW — Hierarchical Advantage Weighting for Online RL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ArXiv ID: 2606.17043
Title: "Hierarchical Advantage Weighting for Online RL Fine-Tuning of VLAs 
        from Sparse Episode Outcomes"
Authors: Tongyan Fang, Siyuan Huang, Naiyu Fang, Ganlong Zhao, Zhongjin Luo, 
         Jianbo Liu, Xiaogang Wang, Ying Dong, Hongsheng Li
Published: June 15, 2026

Abstract:
When pretrained VLA policies are fine-tuned through online RL, each rollout 
episode produces only a single binary outcome (success or failure), yet the 
actor update requires per-transition supervision. Existing approaches commonly 
reduce this sparse outcome to a single scalar reward, but HAW learns to 
distribute the advantage signal hierarchically across tokens.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR YOUR WORK:
├─ "Single binary outcome" = PR merged (1) or rejected (0)
├─ "Per-transition supervision" = which code lines caused rejection?
├─ "Sparse outcome" = you don't have per-line feedback, only PR outcome
└─ HAW solves: "How do I learn from just pass/fail signals?"

#4: Value Axis — Language Models Encode Whether They're on the Right Track
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ArXiv ID: 2606.17056
Title: "The Value Axis: Language Models Encode Whether They're on the Right Track"
Authors: Nick Jiang, Isaac Kauvar, Jack Lindsey
Published: June 15, 2026

Abstract:
We investigate whether language models internally track the value of their 
current trajectory, defined as the likelihood that their ongoing strategy will 
achieve their goals. Using synthetic, in-context reinforcement learning data, 
we construct a "value" axis for Qwen3-8B and find that activations along this 
axis predict whether RL trajectories will succeed.

KEY CONCEPTS TO UNDERSTAND (before reading)
================================================================================

If unfamiliar with terms, here are quick definitions:

CURRICULUM LEARNING:
  Training on easy examples first, then hard ones.
  Why: Teaches the model basic patterns before complex ones.
  Your case: Simple commits first (bugfixes) → complex later (refactors).

SPARSE REWARD REINFORCEMENT LEARNING:
  Training where you only get a signal at the end (pass/fail).
  Why: Simpler than per-token supervision, more realistic.
  Your case: PR merged (reward=1) or rejected (reward=0).

ADVANTAGE WEIGHTING:
  Distributing credit across actions that led to an outcome.
  Why: "Which line in the code caused the PR to fail?"
  Your case: HAW identifies which tokens matter most.

MID-TRAINING:
  Fine-tuning BEFORE RL on a curated supervised dataset.
  Why: Primes the model to generate better trajectories for RL.
  Your case: SFT on merged commits first, then RL on rewards.

VALUE FUNCTION:
  Predicting "will this trajectory succeed?"
  Why: Enables selective sampling (only take confident outputs).
  Your case: "Will this code pass review?" signal.
